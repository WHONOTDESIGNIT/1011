## IPL is safe only when the user is a good candidate

The biggest reason “IPL is safe or not” becomes a problem is not the technology itself. It’s using IPL in the wrong context.

## Avoid or delay IPL when:

### Recent tanning or sunburn
Treating on tanned or sunburned skin increases heat absorption and inflammation risk.

### Broken, irritated, or inflamed skin
Avoid active rashes, open wounds, or irritated areas.

### Tattoos and very dark pigmented spots
Avoid flashing directly over tattoos or very dark moles, because darker pigment absorbs more energy.

### If you are unsure about medication or medical conditions
Some medications can increase photosensitivity, and some medical conditions may change risk. If the user is uncertain, the safest recommendation is to consult a clinician.

## The brand-safe way to present contraindications

Your product pages should include:

- A clear compatibility chart (skin tone + hair color)
- A simple contraindication checklist
- A prompt to stop treatment if burning occurs
