## The short answer: mild and temporary is common, severe and worsening is not

Most safe, expected reactions are **short-lived**. The risk increases when users treat on incompatible skin, use settings too high, or treat too frequently.

## Common, usually normal reactions

- Mild redness that fades within hours
- A warm sensation or light sting during treatment
- Slight swelling around follicles (a “goosebump” look)

## Warning signs (stop and reassess)

- Blistering or scabbing
- Strong swelling that worsens after several hours
- Darkening that increases over days (possible pigment changes)
- Pain that feels like burning during treatment

## What to do if IPL feels too hot

1) Stop the session  
2) Lower intensity next time  
3) Increase time between sessions  
4) Avoid treating on recently tanned skin
