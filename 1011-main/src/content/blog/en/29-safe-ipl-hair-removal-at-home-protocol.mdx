## The safety rule: heat accumulation is what causes problems

Most IPL incidents come from one pattern: **too much heat**, delivered **too quickly**, on skin that is not prepared (tanned, irritated, or incompatible).

This protocol is designed to keep the risk low.

## Step 1: Patch test (always)

Patch testing is the fastest way to validate tolerance for:

- Your skin tone on that body area
- The chosen energy setting
- The effect of recent sun exposure or skincare products

**Patch test checklist**

1) Shave (do not wax or tweeze)  
2) Start at the lowest setting  
3) Treat a small area (about a few cm²)  
4) Wait 24 hours before treating the full area  

If you see blistering, strong swelling, or darkening that worsens, stop and reduce intensity or avoid that area.

## Step 2: Frequency (more is not safer)

Typical safe patterns are:

- Early phase: about once per week for several weeks (follow device instructions)
- Maintenance: every few weeks as needed

More frequent sessions do not necessarily mean faster results, but they can increase irritation and pigment risk.

## Step 3: Settings (ramp up slowly)

Safety-first scaling looks like:

- Start low, especially on sensitive areas
- Increase only after tolerance is clear
- Avoid repeating flashes on the same spot in a single session

## Step 4: Don’t treat on “high risk skin days”

Avoid IPL when:

- Skin is recently tanned or sunburned
- Skin is broken, irritated, or inflamed
- You used strong acids/retinoids recently on that area
