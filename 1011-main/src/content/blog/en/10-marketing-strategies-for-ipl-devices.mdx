## Strategy

### Audience segmentation and positioning

IPL device buyers fall into distinct segments: first-time at-home users seeking convenience, beauty enthusiasts who want salon-grade results, and professional estheticians requiring reliable equipment. Position your device by segment rather than trying to appeal to everyone equally. Technical specifications matter, but ease of use, safety, and visible results drive purchase decisions.

### Influencer partnerships and UGC

Beauty influencers and dermatology content creators provide social proof that transforms browsing into purchasing. Partner with creators who match your target demographic. User-generated content (UGC) showing real before/after results builds trust more effectively than polished brand photography alone.

### Education-led content and trust signals

IPL devices are considered purchases —shoppers research before buying. Create content that answers common questions: "Does IPL hurt?", "Is it safe for my skin tone?", "How long until I see results?" SEO-optimized blog posts, comparison guides, and video demonstrations reduce purchase hesitation and improve conversion rates.

## Channels

### DTC, marketplaces, and retail

A multi-channel approach maximizes reach. Direct-to-consumer (DTC) through your own website offers the highest margins and full brand control. Amazon and other marketplaces provide volume but require competitive pricing and review management. Retail partnerships (beauty supply chains, dermatology clinics) add credibility and offline discovery.

### Performance ads with lifecycle journeys

Run targeted advertising on Meta, Google, and TikTok. Focus prospecting campaigns on education and awareness content. Retarget website visitors with social proof and limited-time offers. Post-purchase campaigns encourage reviews, referrals, and repeat accessory purchases.
