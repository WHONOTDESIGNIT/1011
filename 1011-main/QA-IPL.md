7.Does IPL handset work for my skin tone and hair color?

IPL requires contrast between skin and hair color to work its magic. IPL targets melanin, a hair pigment that absorbs light emitted by the device and directs it to the hair follicle. This means IPL is a good fit for many, but not all skin tones.

IPL may not be effective on very light blonde, red, or grey hair. IPL also may cause burns on highly melanated skin, though it still works for many darker skin tones.

8.Using my IPL handset hurts. What can I do?

If you start feeling burning pain or discomfort, lower the intensity or use the device less frequently. IPL should be a pain-free experience – if you’re experiencing pain or discomfort, here are a few possibilities as to why:

- You have recently been exposed to the sun. We recommend staying out of strong sun exposure for two weeks before using our Handset. 

- Your skin tone is darker than the recommended tones. Please check the skin tone chart on our website or in your user guide. 

- You are using the device at an intensity level that is too high for your skin. We always recommend starting with level 1 intensity to see how your skin reacts. As long as no pain or discomfort occurs, gradually increase the intensity level after each week. 

- You have not shaved first. The IPL Handset may burn long hairs.

- You are pressing down too hard on your skin, causing your skin to get in direct contact with the bulb.

- You are not lifting the handset after each flash. We recommend slightly lifting the Handset after each flash to allow for cool air to cool down the quartz bulb.

- You are using the device on the same spot multiple times per session. We recommend only one pass over each spot, then moving to a new spot.

- You applied a cooling gel, lotion, or any other type of applicant before treatment. We don't recommend using any lotion or ointment before treatment; our handsets are meant to be used without one.

Modes

Lumi has three different modes to make your IPL hair removal journey simpler. Different modes work better for treating different body parts. Here’s how they work:

Stamp Mode:

In Stamp Mode, each click of the pulse button causes one flash.

This is Lumi’s default mode. It’s ideal for precision areas, like your underarms, bikini area, and face. 

Auto Mode:

In Auto Mode, your device will flash every half-second it is in contact with your skin, without needing to click the pulse button.

To activate Auto Mode, hold the device away from your skin and hold down the pulse button until all indicator lights are blinking. Then, hold your handset to your skin and lift the handset after each flash to cool the bulb. To deactivate Auto Mode, hold down the pulse button until the lights stop blinking.

Auto Mode is ideal for treating larger areas, such as legs and arms.

Glide Mode:

In Glide Mode, your device will flash periodically while in contact with your skin, without having to lift the device after each flash.

To activate Glide Mode, make sure the flash window is completely flat against your skin, hold the pulse button down, and glide it across the area you wish to treat.

Glide Mode is ideal for treating legs.
